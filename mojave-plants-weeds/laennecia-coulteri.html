Laennecia coulteri

Coulter's Horsetail

Laennecia coulteri is a perennial herb producing an erect stem with widely spaced leaves, with many narrow, fingerlike lobes. The inflorescences toward the top of the stem each hold three to seven flowers. The flower is very pale to medium pink, sometimes with white streaks, or solid white. It is a tube 2 to 5 centimeters long, opening into a corolla of five twisting, pointed, ribbonlike lobes. The stamens and style do not protrude far from the mouth of the flower, if at all. While it is a perennial plant, it dies after its first flowering.

Plant Data

Names and Taxonomy

  • Common Names: Conyza, Coulter's Horseweed, Coulter's Woolwort, Annual Horsetail
  • Synonyms: Conyza coulteri, Conyza coulteri var. virgata, Eschenbachia coulteri
  • Family: Asteraceae (Sunflower)
  • Genus Information: 6 species in NV, 6 in CA, 6 in US

Habit and Size

  • Duration: annual/weed
  • Habit: herb/forb
  • Shape: upright, columnar
  • Size: 5 ft (1.5 m)
  • Stem: green, hairy

Leaves

  • Color: green
  • Shape: lanceolate, linear
  • Edge: serrated
  • Arrangement: alternate whorls
  • Leaf Retention: deciduous

Flowers and Fruit

  • Sex: monoecious
  • Flower Color: white, pink
  • Flower Size: 1 inch (2 cm)
  • Number of Petals: many
  • Bloom: all year, mostly in fall
  • Fruit: cypsela/achene

Habitat and Growing Conditions

  • Water Use: low
  • Light: full sun
  • Soil: dry
  • Habitat: riparian

Distribution

  • Distribution: AZ, CA, CO, NM, NV, TX, UT
  • Seen: Henderson Bird Viewing Preserve
  • Elevation: 9000 feet (1800)
