Eriogonum deflexum

Skeletonweed

It is somewhat weedy where it is most abundant. This is an annual which varies in size from small patches up to 2 feet on the ground to tangled bushes approaching 6 feet (2 m) in height; it may be dense or thin and spindly. This is a brown or greenish weedy-looking herb with a many-branched stem. The leaves are located at the base of the plant and are rounded and woolly and one to four centimeters long. Small clusters of flowers appear at intervals along the branches with each flower only one to three millimeters wide and white or pinkish in color. The flowers hang downwards. Names and Taxonomy

  • Common Names: Skeletonweed, Flatcrown Buckwheat, Flat-top Buckwheat, Skeletonweed Buckwheat
  • Synonyms: Eriogonum deflexum
  • Family: Polygonaceae (Buckwheat)

Habit and Size

  • Duration: annual
  • Habit: forb
  • Shape: triangular
  • Size: up to 6 feet, usually up to 1 foot
  • Stem: green

Leaves

  • Color: green above, white below
  • Shape: round to kidney shaped and wooly
  • Edge: smooth
  • Arrangement: basal
  • Leaf Retention: drought deciduous

Flowers and Fruit

  • Sex: monoecious
  • Flower Color: white, red, pink, green
  • Flower Size: 1/4 inch
  • Number of Petals: 4
  • Bloom: all year
  • Fruit: achene

Habitat and Growing Conditions

  • Water Use: low
  • Light: full sun
  • Soil: well drained
  • Habitat: sandy, gravel, disturbed areas

Distribution

  • Distribution: AZ, CA, NM, NV, UT
  • Seen: Las Vegas Wash
  • Elevation: Below 5200 feet
