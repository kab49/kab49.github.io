Chylismia claviformis

Browneyes

It is an annual plant growing from a basal rosette of long oval leaves and producing stems often exceeding half a meter in height. Atop the stem is an inflorescence of one to many primrose blooms, each with four white or yellow petals. The pistil may be quite long and has a bulbous stigma at the tip. The stamens are somewhat shorter and they bear long hairy anthers containing white or yellow pollen. The floral axis at the junction of male and female parts is bright red to maroon or brown. This species is found across western North America from the Pacific Northwest to northern Mexico.

Names and Taxonomy

  • Common Names: Browneyes, Brown-eyed Primrose, Clavatefruit Suncup
  • Synonyms: Camissonia claviformis (Torr. & Frém.) P.H.Raven, Oenothera claviformis Torr. & Frém., Camissonia claviformis, Oenothera scapoidea var. claviformis, Chylismia scapoidea var. claviformis
  • Family: Onagraceae (Evening Primrose)
  • Genus Information: 16 species of Camissonia in Mojave Desert and North America. There are many subspecies.
