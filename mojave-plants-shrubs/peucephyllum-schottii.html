Peucephyllum schottii

Schott’s Pygmy Cedar

It is not a cedar, fir, or pine, but a member of the aster family, Asteraceae. It is a leafy evergreen shrub with glandular, resinous foliage. Dark green leaves are absent below (denuded) and leafy above. The leaves are like fir-needles or narrowly linear, gland-dotted. It flowers in yellow flower heads which have only disc florets. Blooms are often triggered by rainfall, particularly after summer and winter rainfall. The fruits are woody, bristly seeds with a pappus.

Plant Data

Names and Taxonomy

  • Common Names: Schott’s Pygmy Cedar, Pygmy Cedar, Desert Fir, Desert Pine
  • Synonyms: Peucephyllum schottii
  • Family: Asteraceae (Aster or Sunflower)

Habit and Size

  • Duration: perennial
  • Habit: shrub
  • Shape: bush
  • Size: up to 6 feet
  • Stem: green

Leaves

  • Color: green
  • Shape: narrowly linear
  • Edge: smooth
  • Arrangement: alternate
  • Leaf Retention: evergreen

Flowers and Fruit

  • Sex: monoecious
  • Flower Color: yellow
  • Flower Size: 1/4 inch
  • Number of Petals: none
  • Bloom: Dec, Jan, Feb, Mar, Apr, May, Jun
  • Fruit: seeds with a pappous

Habitat and Growing Conditions

  • Water Use: Deep, infrequent watering is best
  • Light: full sun
  • Soil: well drained
  • Habitat: Dry rocky slopes, washes, desert scrubs

Distribution

  • Distribution: AZ, CA, NV, UT
  • Seen: Boulder Beach, Lake Mead
  • Elevation: 450-4200 ft (150-1400 m)
