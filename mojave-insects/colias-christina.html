Colias christina

Christina Sulphur

Introduction:

It has slight orange-red ground color and is conspicuous by the yellow basal part of both wings. The underside has no markings except the median spot. The female is almost white, its forewing with only a little dark dusting at the distal margin and black, white-centred median spot. Flight period is from May until September. The Montana field guide states that this butterfly is nonmigratory.
Life Cycle:

Size wingspan 1.4-2 inches (35 to 52 mm)
Sexual Dimorphism: true
Metamorphosis: complete (egg, larva, pupa, adult)

Insect Data

Names and Taxonomy

  • Common Names: Christina Sulphur
  • Synonyms: Eurymus christina, 2 subspecies
  • Order: Lepidoptera (Butterflies and Moths)
  • Family: Pieridae (Whites and Sulfers)
  • Genus Information: 4 species in NV, 6 in CA, 16 in US

Distribution

  • Distribution: MT, NV, UT, WY western Canada
  • Seen: Henderson Bird Viewing Preserve
  • Habitat: meadows, sagebrush flats, and along woodland trails and clearings
  • Food: Larvae feed on Trifolium and Hedysarum species. Adults on nectar.
